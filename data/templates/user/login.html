There are now several options for logging in to the Gemini Observatory Archive - the "traditional" GOA username and password options still works, but you can now also authenticate using your ORCID ID, and NOIRlab staff can authenticate using NOIRlab single sign on.

Whichever method you use to log in, you should end up logged in to the same GOA account. Please try to avoid ending up with different accounts for different sign-on methods, as things like proprietary program access will then be inconsistent between your accounts. The key here is the email address associated with your various sign-ons.

If for example you already have a traditional GOA username and password, and you would like so switch to signing in with your ORCID, please ensure that the email address you have registered to your GOA account matches the one you have registered to your ORCID account. You can update the email address on your GOA account here".

NORlab staff users - everything above regarding ORCID sign-in also applies to NOIRLab SSO sign in. Please also note that gemini.edu and noirlab.edu email addresses are not considered to be the same in this case.

Not sure which to use?

  • If you are a Gemini User (i.e. research astronomer or other community member accessing Gemini data), we recommend using ORCID. This applies even if you are also NOIRlab staff.
    • If you don't already have an ORCID ID, sign up for one here.
    • If you already have a GOA account, but it uses a different email address to your ORCID, please go here to update your GOA email to match your ORCID email before signing in with ORCID.
    • If you don't already have a GOA account, just sign in using ORCID, we'll set one up for you automatically as sign in.
  • If you are NOIRlab staff member, do not have an ORCID, and are accessing GOA for operational duties, use the NOIRlab SSO sign in.
    • If you don't already have an account, we'll create one automatically for as you sign in.
    • If you need staff data access privelidges, you will need to request that separately after you have signed.
  • If you are not a NOIRlab staff member, don't want to create or use an ORCID, or just prefer the simple old ways, sign in using the traditional username / password form. If you don't already have an account, you can request one here
  • If you think you've ended up with multiple accounts, please either file a helpdesk ticket, or contact Paul Hirst, and it should be simple for us to merge your accounts into one.
